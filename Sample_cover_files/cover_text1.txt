Steganography is the practice of hiding secret information inside another ordinary file, message, image, audio, or video in such a way that the existence of the hidden information is not obvious. The word steganography comes from the Greek words steganos, meaning “covered”, and graphein, meaning “writing”. Therefore, steganography literally means “covered writing”. Unlike cryptography, which focuses on protecting the content of a message by encrypting it, steganography focuses on concealing the presence of the message itself.

The idea of steganography has existed for centuries. In ancient Greece, messages were sometimes written on wooden tablets and then covered with wax so that the secret message remained hidden beneath the surface. Another historical technique involved tattooing messages on a messenger’s shaved head and allowing the hair to grow back before sending the person to deliver the message.

In the digital age, steganography has become more sophisticated and widely used. Digital steganography commonly hides data inside multimedia files such as images, audio, and video. For example, a secret message can be hidden within the pixels of an image by slightly modifying the least significant bits of the pixel values. These modifications are usually so small that they cannot be detected by the human eye.

Steganography can also be applied to audio files by embedding hidden data into sound samples without noticeably affecting the quality of the audio. Similarly, video steganography hides information across multiple frames of a video file. Text steganography, another method, hides information within text files by manipulating spaces, formatting, or character placement.

Today, steganography is used in various fields including digital watermarking, copyright protection, secure communication, and information security. However, it can also be misused for illegal purposes such as hiding malicious information. Because of this, researchers continue to develop techniques to detect and prevent hidden data, a process known as steganalysis.
